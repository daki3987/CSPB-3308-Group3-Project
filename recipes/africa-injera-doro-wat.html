Injera with Doro Wat (Ethiopia)

A traditional Ethiopian dish featuring spongy fermented flatbread (injera) served with a spicy chicken stew called doro wat. Rich in flavor and cultural heritage, it's a beloved meal often shared during gatherings.

Ingredients
  • 2 cups teff flour
  • 2.5 cups water (for injera batter)
  • 8 chicken drumsticks or thighs
  • 2 large onions, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp ginger, minced
  • 3 tbsp berbere spice
  • 1/4 cup niter kibbeh (spiced clarified butter)
  • 4 hard-boiled eggs
  • Salt to taste
Instructions
  1. Mix teff flour and water in a bowl. Cover and ferment for 2-3 days at room temperature.
  2. Stir the fermented batter, pour into a hot non-stick pan, cook one side only to make injera. Set aside.
  3. Sauté onions in niter kibbeh until soft and golden.
  4. Add garlic, ginger, and berbere. Cook for 5 minutes.
  5. Add chicken and coat with the mixture. Cook for 10 minutes.
  6. Pour in water, cover, and simmer for 30-40 minutes until chicken is tender.
  7. Add boiled eggs during the last 5 minutes of cooking.
  8. Serve doro wat hot over injera.
